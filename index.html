Description

The school is an integral part of the Nirimba District. In addition to the regular school curriculum, the school provides several unique programs which cater for the needs of our students. The school enjoys a strong reputation in creative arts, sport and gifted and talented education. Our students and staff live by our school motto - Aim High. The school's expectations of being Motivated, Respectful, Proud and Safe underpin everything we do. The school strives to ensure that all children have the confidence to learn by providing positive, caring teaching, through a diverse and integrated curriculum which attempts to cater for a wide range of both society's and children's needs in a pleasant and ordered environment. Students are encouraged to invest their best efforts in their learning. Community involvement plays an important role in instilling the school values to aim high and strive for excellence.

History

Our School History

Metella Road Public School was established in 1972 but was not officially opened till the 5th June 1982 by The Minister of Education the 'Hon. R. J. Mulock'. Metella Road Public School forms part of the Nirimba group of schools and is supported by the Western Sydney Regional office. Metella Road Public School has a long and proud tradition of providing quality public education to the Toongabbie area for over 40 years. In policy, programs, history and culture, Metella Road Public School is underpinned by a tradition of excellence. This tradition has fostered high community expectations and a correspondingly high level of dedication and commitment by staff members. Metella Road Public School has a clear focus on providing quality educational opportunities for each child. The school provides programs to identify and assist children who have learning difficulties and the needs of talented students are addressed by enrichment programs. There is a high level of parent participation in the school with a large band of volunteers whose willing assistance greatly enhances the opportunities available to students.
